Overview Research Solution & PRD
10 interviews qualitative synthesis • themes • user needs

People don’t hate discovery — they hate the effort.

I interviewed 10 Spotify users (friends/family) across listening styles to understand when discovery fails, what users avoid, and what would make exploring feel easy.

Method

  • 10 qualitative interviews (10–15 min each)
  • Mix of daily listeners, casual listeners, and enthusiasts
  • Focus: discovery habits, pain points, and “why” behind avoidance

What I looked for

  • Where users currently discover music (surfaces & moments)
  • What causes overwhelm or disengagement
  • How users evaluate “good recommendations”
  • Whether feedback actions feel effective

Top themes

Discovery avoidance was driven by friction, not lack of interest.

Familiarity wins Discovery feels like work Too much choice Low trust in feedback loop

Key insights

1) Users default to trusted playlists

Most users rely on a small rotation (Daily Mix, Liked Songs, old playlists) because it’s predictable and low-risk.

2) Discovery feels like a commitment

Clicking discovery surfaces often implies time, attention, and patience — which users don’t always have.

3) Choice overload leads to no action

Too many entry points can create paralysis; users pick the familiar option instead.

4) Feedback doesn’t feel like it “works”

Users aren’t confident that likes/skips materially improve recommendations, reducing motivation to engage.

Interview quotes (anonymized)

These are representative quotes from the 10 interviews (friends/family). Names are anonymized by role.

“I know what I’m getting with my Daily Mix. I don’t want to gamble on something new.”
A Daily listener • mid-20s
“If I click Discover Weekly, I feel like I have to actually sit and listen. Sometimes I just want easy.”
B Casual listener • 30s
“There are so many playlists that I don’t know where to start… so I don’t.”
C Background listener • 40s
“I skip a ton, but I’m not sure Spotify learns from it. It still gives me stuff I don’t like.”
D Daily listener • 20s
“I’d explore more if it was like a quick sample, not a whole playlist commitment.”
E Explorer • late-20s
“When I’m driving, I can’t make choices. I need Spotify to just give me something safe-but-new.”
F Commuter listener • 30s
“I only try new music when a friend sends it. Otherwise I stay in my comfort zone.”
G Social discovery • 20s
“Sometimes recommendations feel repetitive. Like… I already heard these songs a hundred times.”
H Power user • 20s

Synthesized user need

Users want a low-friction way to sample new music that feels lightweight, doesn’t require commitment, and builds trust that feedback improves recommendations.
