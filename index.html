Make discovery feel effortless — not like a commitment.

This case study proposes a lightweight discovery experience for Spotify users who love music but default to familiar playlists because discovery feels overwhelming, repetitive, or too high-effort.

10 user interviews Clear non-goals MVP scoped for A/B test Success metrics defined

Problem statement

Spotify has powerful discovery surfaces, but users often disengage because exploring new music can feel like a decision-heavy commitment. We need a low-friction way to sample new music without overwhelm.

Designed as a realistic APM-level PRD: UX-led, measurable, and scoped to ship.

Why this matters

When discovery feels too effortful, users revert to the same trusted playlists. That reduces exploration, limits taste-building, and can flatten long-term engagement. A small interaction shift can unlock meaningful gains without requiring new recommendation infrastructure.

  • Discovery drives retention and satisfaction
  • New artist engagement benefits the ecosystem
  • Lower cognitive load increases exploration

Goals

Increase discovery engagement while reducing cognitive load and decision fatigue.

Non-goals (scope discipline)

  • Social sharing or friend feeds
  • Artist-side tooling changes
